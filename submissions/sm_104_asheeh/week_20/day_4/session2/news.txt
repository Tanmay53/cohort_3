How worried should you be about the coronavirus?How worried should I be?  It's a complicated question for two reasons. First, much remains unknown about COVID-19. Many cases are thought to be asymptomatic making it hard to gauge its spread or how deadly it is. Second, much of the risk comes not from the virus itself but from how it affects the societies it hits given the financial inequality of our world.Science
Tourism ministry tracking 450 Iranian tourists who entered India in FebThe tourism ministry is tracking the whereabouts of 450 Iranian tourists who entered the country in February this year, in view of the spread of coronavirus in their home country, senior officials said. Iran has the third-highest number of COVID-19 deaths, standing at 124. These tourists are only persons of interest and are not confirmed carriers of the virus.India
Juvenile board convicts two minors for lynching Pehlu KhanIn the first conviction for the horrific lynching of Pehlu Khan in 2017, two minors have been found guilty in the case by a Juvenile Justice Board in Alwar, police said. Sentencing will be pronounced on Saturday. The six accused - Vipin Yadav, Ravindra Kumar, Kaluram, Dayanand, Yogesh Kumar and Bheem Rathi - were given the benefit of doubt and were acquitted.City
IND vs SA: Team will take precautionary measure, but SA tour onThe South African team will take all the necessary precautions, but will proceed with its forthcoming India tour as the risk of the deadly novel Coronavirus (COVID-19) is "low" in Delhi and Dubai at the moment, Cricket South Africa (CSA) said in a statement on Friday. South Africa will play three ODIs in India. The first match is in Dharamshala on March 12.Sports

Widespread rains lash Punjab, Haryana; crop loss assessment orderedWidespread rains lashed Punjab and Haryana on Friday, triggering fear among farmers about damage to wheat crop almost a month before the harvesting season. "Rains are not good at this stage and it will certainly affect the yield of wheat," Bhartiya Kisan Union (Ekta Ugrahan) general secretary Sukhdev Singh said. The Punjab govt ordered an assessment of crop loss.India
RBI to look into what went wrong at Yes Bank: Nirmala SitharamanAddressing a press conference after the RBI superseded the board of Yes Bank and placed withdrawal restrictions, finance minister Nirmala Sitharaman on Friday said the govt has asked RBI to look into what went wrong at Yes Bank. Since 2017, RBI noticed governance issues, weak regulatory compliance, besides wrong asset classification and risky credit decisions.Business
Air pollution in Delhi-NCR: We are worried for future, says Supreme Court"We are worried for future", the Supreme Court said on Friday, asking the municipal corporations and other agencies in Delhi to consider purchasing BS-VI compliant diesel vehicles to reduce pollution. The SC bench was hearing a plea seeking permission to allow registration of several diesel vehicles of 2000cc capacity and above to carry out solid waste management.City
'Right talking' critics hate those doing right things: PM ModiSpeaking at the ET Global Business Summit, PM Modi on Friday hit out at 'right talking' critics of his govt's decisions such as the CAA and scrapping of special status for J&K, saying they have hatred for people who walk the path of 'doing the right things'. The PM said his govt has the "conviction to do the right things, the conviction to break the status quo."India
Global GDP may lose $77-347 billion due to coronavirusThe coronavirus outbreak has the potential to significantly harm the Asian economies, and the global economy may suffer losses of $77-347 billion, Asian Development Bank (ADB) said on Friday. The virus outbreak may impact developing Asian economies through sharp declines in domestic demand, lower tourism and business travel, trade and production linkages.Business

NIA arrests two more people in Pulwama attack caseTwo more persons, including the one who procured chemicals online for making the IED used in the Pulwama attack, were arrested by the NIA in the case on Friday, an official said. Forty CRPF personnel were killed in the deadly terror attack in south Kashmir's Pulwama district last year after a suicide bomber rammed an explosive-laden car in the CRPF convoy.India
How deadly is the coronavirus?The count of deaths reported worldwide suggests COVID-19 may be more deadly than the flu, as per the head of the WHO. Globally, about 3.4% of reported COVID-19 cases have died. By comparison, seasonal flu generally kills far fewer than 1% of those infected. However, it's far too soon to predict whether it ultimately winds up worse than flu or about the same.India
Nirbhaya case convict moves SC seeking restoration of his legal remediesMukesh Singh, one of the four death row convicts in the Nirbhaya gang-rape and murder case, moved a plea in the SC on Friday seeking restoration of all his legal remedies, alleging that his lawyers misled him. The plea, filed through advocate ML Sharma, sought a CBI probe into alleged "criminal conspiracy" hatched by the Centre, Delhi govt and the amicus curiae.City
Depositors' money safe: Nirmala Sitharaman on Yes Bank crisis"I'm in continuous interaction with the RBI. The central bank is fully seized of the matter and has assured they will give a quick resolution. I want to assure every depositor that their money shall be safe," Sitharaman said a day after the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) placed cash-strapped Yes Bank under moratorium.Business
When will there be a coronavirus treatment?At a White House meeting with President Trump, executives from the world's biggest drug companies said they were making progress but antiviral medicines could be available in months, with a vaccine only by next year. While drugmakers are racing to find treatments, the history isn't encouraging given little progress scientists could make in case of Ebola and Zika.India
